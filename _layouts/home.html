When humans speak, they encode spontaneously in the acoustic signals they produce complex information about the linguistic content they communicate, their emotions, physiological, and social characteristics.

My aim is to identify automatic markers in speech and language that can explain how language fails in the brain. To this end, I combine natural language processing, acoustic analysis, human neuroimaging, and machine learning.

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Department of Neurology, School of Medicine, Johns Hopkins University, Baltimore, USA; I am also a researcher at Språkbanken, Department of Swedish, University of Gothenburg, Sweden.

In my earlier positions, I was a postdoctoral fellow at CLASP at the University of Gothenburg in Sweden. Before that I have been a visiting scholar at the Seeger Center for Hellenic Studies, Princeton University and a temporary Lecturer at the Department of English Studies, University of Cyprus.
